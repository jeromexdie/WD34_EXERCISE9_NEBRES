Appetizers

SPINACI ZOLA DIP

The appetizer gets its name from its two primary ingredients: spinach and gorgonzola. Both fresh and frozen spinach will work well with this recipe— the latter saves time by skipping the blanching step. If you can’t get your hands on gorgonzola, use any blue cheese in its place.

BRUSCHETTA POMODORO

A classic appetizer: mixed tasty bruschettas with a platter of cold cuts and cheese! It may seem obvious to you to prepare perfect tomato bruschettas, but even this simple recipe has its secrets. In addition to the choice of bread, homemade, preparing the tomatoes seasoned with basil, oil and oregano ahead of time will ensure an even tastier and more fragrant result, a delicious sauce that will further flavor the toasted slices of bread.

Soups

SUMMER SQUASH CREMA DI ZUCCA.PANCETTA.

This creamy pumpkin soup, known in Italy as Crema Di Zucca, is a simple and delicious celebration of fall bounty. Think of this recipe as a base, too - it's delicious as is in its simplicity, but you can have some fun doctoring it up with your favorite warming spices as well. Curry powder, allspice, a little extra nutmeg and cinnamon, cumin and more are all great additions.

TOMATO PAPPA AL POMODORO

This simple tomato, bread and basil soup hails from Tuscany, a region renowned for its soups. As simple as it sounds, this quick and easy dish delivers bags of flavour and makes a great vegan lunch or starter. Be sure to use good quality extra virgin olive oil – it makes all the difference.

Pasta

RAVIOLI SPINACH RICOTTA

Creamy cheese and spinach ravioli is smothered in a silky, rich sauce for a grown-up version of a classic favorite pasta dish. The easy homemade alfredo-style sauce is mixed with spinach and cheese ravioli for a luscious pasta dish your family will love. Plus, it all cooks in one skillet.

SHRIMP ANCHOVY LINGUINE ALLA SICILIANA.

Pasta alla Siciliana is a traditional Sicilian pasta dish and a specialty of Messina. It’s made with linguine or spaghetti, and a collection of powerful ingredients. It combines the typical sweet, spicy and salty tastes of the Sicilian traditional food. The dish is very simple and takes only a few minutes of preparation. Its success depends crucially on the quality of the ingredients. The magic comes with the tastiness of the olive oil, the dried tomatoes, the olives, and the capers.

Oval Pizza Calzone

TOMATO.BASIL. PIZZA MARGHERITA.

Pizza margherita, as the Italians call it, is a simple pizza hailing from Naples. When done right, margherita pizza features a bubbly crust, crushed San Marzano tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella and basil, a drizzle of olive oil, and a sprinkle of salt. That is all. Sure, you could add some tomato if you’d like, but only if the tomato is truly ripe, red and worthy. Authentic Neapolitan pizza margherita doesn’t require fresh tomato. Try making this pizza as written, and you won’t miss the tomato on top. Sometimes less is more.
